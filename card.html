Highland Cow

Highland cows are almost as cold-tolerant as the arctic-dwelling caribou and reindeer.

  • Country of Origin: Scotland
  • Weight: Male: 800 kg, Female: 500 kg
  • Height: Male: 3.5-4 ft, Female: 3-3.5 ft
  • Cattle: Bos primigenius taurus

Highland cattle are a Scottish cattle breed. They have long horns and long wavy coats that are coloured black, brindle, red, yellow, white or silver. They originated in the Highlands and Western Isles of Scotland and were first mentioned in the 6th century AD. The first herd book described two distinct types of Highland cattle but, due to crossbreeding between the two, only one type now exists.
